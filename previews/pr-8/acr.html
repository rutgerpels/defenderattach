How to use this dashboard

A plain-English field guide for sales follow-up conversations — everything the dashboard calculates, in one place.

What it shows Which view to use Urgency split Servers & Storage gap Low-priority workloads

What this dashboard shows

Each account buys Azure workloads — containers, SQL, App Service, storage, servers and more — without always having the matching Defender for Cloud plan switched on. The Overview cards size the total monthly and annualized attach gap across the book, how many accounts are affected, and which Defender service carries the largest gap. Use the tabs to move from the book-level picture to a single-account story in Customer Drill-Down.

Which view should I use?

Service Attach Opportunities

Use this when you want to know what protection to attach next. It finds customers who already buy Azure services but do not appear to have the matching Defender plan turned on.

  • Sales example: The customer spends on SQL databases, containers, or App Service, but the matching Defender coverage is missing or much lower than expected.
  • Use it for: building account call lists, attach motions, and concrete "you already run this, let's protect it" conversations.
  • Question it answers: "What should we sell or attach next?"

Defender Coverage Drift

Use this when you want to understand whether protection is keeping pace as the customer's Azure footprint changes over time.

  • Sales example: Azure usage is growing month over month, but Defender for Cloud is flat, shrinking, or growing much more slowly.
  • Use it for: account health reviews, renewal conversations, and explaining why security coverage may be falling behind cloud growth.
  • Question it answers: "Where is protection not keeping up with Azure growth?"
Simple rule: Start with Service Attach Opportunities when you need a sales action list. Use Defender Coverage Drift when you need a trend story that explains why an account may be under-protected over time.

Reading the urgency split (Accelerating vs Stable)

The urgency bar on the Overview splits the same total attach gap by momentum — it does not add anything on top:

  • Accelerating — the share of the gap on workloads growing faster than Defender is keeping up (drawn from Defender Coverage Drift). These are the most time-sensitive dollars.
  • Stable — the remaining gap on workloads that are flat.
No double counting: Accelerating + Stable always equals the single headline total. Coverage drift re-segments that total by urgency; it is never summed on top of it. The amber drift-risk chip flags services that already meet the benchmark (so they carry no current dollar gap) but where Defender is shrinking or lagging the workload — a watch list, not part of the dollar total.

How the Servers & Storage gap is estimated

Why these two are different

Most Defender plans (SQL, Containers, App Service, Key Vault…) are billed roughly as a percentage of the Azure service they protect, so we compare actual Defender spend to that expected percentage directly.

  • Defender for Servers is priced per server, per hour — not as a share of your VM bill.
  • Defender for Storage is priced per storage account plus per million transactions — not as a share of your storage bill.
  • Because there is no fixed percentage, an earlier version left these out of the dollar gap, which made the headline look lower than reality.

How we estimate the gap now

We benchmark each account against peers who already protect the same workload:

  • Take every customer who already buys the plan and work out what share of their VM (or Storage) spend goes to that Defender plan.
  • Use the typical (median) peer ratio — usually in the low-to-mid single digits of percent. If there are too few peers, we fall back to a conservative 6% benchmark.
  • Apply that ratio to this customer's VM or Storage spend to get an expected Defender spend, then gap = expected − what they actually spend (never below zero).
Worked example: A customer spends $27,000/mo on Storage with $0 Defender for Storage. At a ~5.7% peer benchmark, expected Defender spend is about $1,540/mo, so the estimated attach gap is roughly $1,540/mo (about $18.5k/yr). This is a directional, medium-confidence estimate to prioritise the conversation — confirm the actual server and storage counts before quoting.

Why some small workloads show as Low priority

When a workload has no measurable Defender coverage we still flag it, but we only escalate it to High/Medium once the spend is big enough to be worth a call. That bar is tuned per service’s cost: roughly $500/mo for big-ticket services (Servers, Storage, Containers, SQL), $250/mo for mid-tier data & app services, and just $50/mo for inherently cheap services like Key Vault. Below its threshold a service stays Low (monitor only) so the High list reflects real, sizeable opportunities. This only affects prioritisation — it never changes the dollar gap.
